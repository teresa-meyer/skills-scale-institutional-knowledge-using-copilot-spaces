# OctoAcme Project Management Documentation

Welcome to the OctoAcme project management documentation hub. This README is the starting point for new and onboarding team members to quickly understand how projects are initiated, planned, executed, released, and continuously improved across the team.

OctoAcme uses a lightweight but structured project management approach that spans the full project lifecycle from initiation through planning, execution, release, and retrospective improvement. Work begins with a clear business need, defined success metrics, stakeholder alignment, and a lightweight one-pager that captures goals, risks, milestones, and team roles. Once approved, planning focuses on breaking work into shippable increments, prioritizing a backlog, estimating scope, documenting acceptance criteria and Definition of Done, and identifying dependencies and release milestones. This creates a repeatable framework that keeps projects aligned to measurable outcomes while remaining practical for cross-functional delivery.

The process is supported by clearly defined roles and shared ownership across the team. Project Managers coordinate timelines, risks, delivery rituals, and stakeholder communication, while Product Managers define priorities, outcomes, and success measures. Developers are responsible for implementation, testing, technical collaboration, and surfacing engineering risks, and QA or testing contributors validate acceptance criteria and overall quality. Stakeholders also play an important role by providing input, approvals, and direction at key decision points. Together, these personas create a model where planning, execution, and accountability are distributed but well coordinated.

Communication is treated as an operational discipline throughout the process. OctoAcme emphasizes regular team rhythms including standups, weekly syncs, sprint or milestone demos, PM–Product alignment meetings, and monthly stakeholder updates. Risks and blockers are documented in a risk register, reviewed regularly, and escalated through a defined path from team-level triage to PM, Product Lead, and sponsor-level escalation when business impact increases. Weekly status updates, incident communications, and single sources of truth such as project documentation or release notes help maintain transparency and reduce confusion across teams.

Quality assurance is embedded throughout execution and release rather than treated as a final checkpoint. During delivery, the team uses project boards, small pull requests, linked issues, acceptance criteria, automated CI checks, and required review approvals to support consistent execution. Testing expectations include unit tests for new logic, integration tests where needed, end-to-end smoke tests for critical flows, security scanning in CI, and manual QA for feature acceptance when appropriate. Before release, OctoAcme requires completed acceptance criteria, passing CI and security scans, drafted release notes, prepared rollback plans, and post-deployment verification. After each sprint, release, or incident, retrospectives capture lessons learned and convert them into tracked action items, reinforcing a culture of continuous improvement.
